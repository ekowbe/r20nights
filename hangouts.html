Midweek · On Your Campus

What a hangout's
actually like.

Saturday night is the easy place to start. This is what comes next — a midweek hangout on your own campus, usually a friend bringing you along once you already know a few faces. Smaller, chiller, zero pressure. Here's exactly what it's like, so none of it feels like a mystery.

Who
~10 students
Your campus. Mostly people who came alone the first time.
When
Midweek nights
Around an hour. Come late, leave early — no one minds.
Where
A dorm lounge
Someone's common room. Couches, not pews.
What
Talk it out
We read a bit, then talk about it — and whatever else is going on.

You don't have to believe anything to show up.

There's no sign-in, no putting you on the spot, no weird icebreaker where you have to say something spiritual. People ask real questions. People disagree. Someone usually brings snacks. If you want to just listen the whole time, that's completely normal.

Most of us walked in the first time not knowing a single person. An hour later it didn't feel like that anymore.

See for yourself.

Start with Saturday.

The easiest way in is just showing up to a Night — no hangout required, no one singles you out, slip in and out as you like. Come once, meet a few people, and the campus hangouts open up from there.
